## 2. Egypt, Lapis, and Sapir

### Egyptian word *iwn* (color = character/essence)
- R. Faulkner, *Concise Dictionary of Middle Egyptian* (s.v. *iwn*)
- A. Ritner, *The Mechanics of Ancient Egyptian Magical Practice* (color and sacredness)
- General works on Egyptian color symbolism

### Lapis lazuli in Egypt
- GIA, *Lapis Lazuli History and Lore*
- A. Lucas, *Ancient Egyptian Materials and Industries*
- Recent Egyptological color-studies on stone-working and imported blue stones (e.g., electrummagazine.com)

### Egyptian blue (khesbedj-irtyt)
- Standard Egyptian-technology surveys treating Egyptian blue
- I. Freestone, M. Meeks, and M. Sax — related pigment studies

### Sapir = lapis lazuli, not corundum sapphire
- Theophrastus, *On Stones* §58 (sappheiros "speckled with gold")
- Smith, *Dictionary of the Bible* (s.v. "Sapphire") — distinction between ancient sapir and modern sapphire
- HALOT, Huehnergard, von Soden — Hebrew-Akkadian-Greek lexica treating sapir as lapis lazuli
- "Tekhelet: Setting the Ancient Record Straight" (Tradition Online)

### No sapphire in ancient Egypt (absence-of-evidence claim)
> "No verified blue-corundum (sapphire) object has been identified in pre-Roman Egyptian archaeological contexts; see A. Lucas, *Ancient Egyptian Materials*, and modern Egyptological color-studies that note the absence of sapphire and the dominance of lapis, turquoise, and other stones."

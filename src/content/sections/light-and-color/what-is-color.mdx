<p class="bt">Color is not a property of objects. It is a property of light. Specifically, color is a wavelength of electromagnetic radiation that falls within the range our eyes can detect. Different wavelengths of light produce different color sensations in the eye.</p>

<TermDef word="Wavelength">Light travels as a wave — an oscillating electromagnetic field moving through space. The wavelength is the distance from one wave crest to the next. It is measured in nanometers (nm) — billionths of a meter. Shorter wavelength = the wave oscillates more rapidly. Longer wavelength = the wave oscillates more slowly.</TermDef>

<TermDef word="Frequency">The number of wave cycles passing a fixed point per second. Frequency and wavelength are directly related: shorter wavelength = higher frequency. Higher frequency means more energy per photon. Violet light has shorter wavelength and higher energy than red light. This energy difference is what makes violet and red light interact differently with matter — and why they appear as different colors.</TermDef>

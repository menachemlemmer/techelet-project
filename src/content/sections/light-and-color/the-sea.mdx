Water itself is nearly colorless. A glass of water held up to light is transparent. The dominant color of the sea at any moment comes from something else entirely: it reflects the sky.

The surface of calm water acts as a mirror. It reflects the color of whatever is above it. A grey overcast sky produces a grey sea. A vivid blue sky produces a vivid blue sea. When you look at the blue sea, you are primarily seeing the blue sky — redirected downward by the water's surface and back up to your eye.

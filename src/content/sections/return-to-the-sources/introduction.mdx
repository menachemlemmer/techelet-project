<p class="bt">In the previous movement we built the chemistry of tekhelet from the ground up — the chromophore, the hydrogen bond lattice, the two keys, the bromine shield, the three indigoid compounds. We did this without reference to the Talmudic sources. Now we return to the sources. What we will find is that the Sages, working without access to this chemistry, encoded a precision that the chemistry now explains.</p>

<p class="bt">We begin with Shabbat 75a — the Shabbat prohibitions involved in producing tekhelet, and why the living state of the chilazon is not merely preferred but required. We then turn to Menachot 42b — Abaye's question about how tekhelet is dyed, and what the yora was doing. Finally, Menachot 43a — the two authentication tests, and what they were actually probing at the molecular level.</p>
